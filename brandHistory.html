Brand History: Pashley Cycles - Hand-Built in England

...

Founded by William "Rath" Pashley in the idyllic town of Stratford-upon-Avon, Pashley Cycles initially focused on the production of carrier cycles and tricycles for the local delivery market. However, it wasn't long before the company expanded its offerings to include a wide range of bicycles catering to various needs and preferences.

Throughout the decades, Pashley Cycles has remained steadfast in its commitment to traditional manufacturing techniques, ensuring that each bicycle is meticulously crafted by skilled artisans. The company's dedication to hand-built quality has garnered it a reputation for excellence, with cyclists worldwide seeking out Pashley bicycles for their unmatched durability and craftsmanship.

One of Pashley's most iconic models is the "Pashley Princess," a classic ladies' bicycle characterized by its elegant curves and timeless design. Originally introduced in the 1950s, the Princess has since become a symbol of British cycling heritage, beloved by riders for its comfort and style.

In addition to its classic models, Pashley Cycles has also embraced innovation, incorporating modern technology and materials to enhance performance while staying true to its heritage. Despite these advancements, the company remains committed to preserving the timeless appeal and hand-built craftsmanship that have defined its brand for nearly a century.

Today, Pashley Cycles continues to thrive as a symbol of British cycling excellence, with its bicycles cherished by riders of all ages and backgrounds. Whether navigating bustling city streets or exploring scenic countryside roads, Pashley bicycles embody the perfect blend of tradition, quality, and style, making them a beloved choice for cyclists around the world.
